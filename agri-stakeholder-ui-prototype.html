Agriculture Stakeholder Interface — Design Prototype

A simple, accessible HTML prototype mapping stakeholder needs to interface features. Click the tabs to explore each stakeholder's tailored view.

Agricultural Experts

Description: Field scientists, crop pathologists and extension specialists who validate knowledge and ensure system accuracy.

Requirements
  • Easy submission and review of disease cases (images + notes)
  • Peer review and consensus controls for labels
  • Tools for accuracy tracking and error reporting
  • Clear provenance and source metadata for each record
Functions
  • Upload case (images, GPS, short symptom form)
  • Annotate images and mark regions of interest
  • Accept/reject labels with reason codes
  • Run quick validation tests against model predictions
Interface Features
Case Review Panel

Large image with zoom, annotation tools, provenance header (who submitted, when, location).

Consensus Queue

List of cases needing 2+ expert reviews with clear accept/reject buttons and short justification field.

Accuracy Monitor

Simple dashboard showing model precision/recall by crop and disease with trend sparklines.

Export & Report

Export validated cases (CSV, JSON) with metadata for research or policy use.

Quick mock controls

Government & Extension Officers

Description: Policy makers and field officers who promote technology adoption and sustainable practices.

Requirements
  • Simple, action-oriented insights for farmers
  • Local adoption metrics and demonstration results
  • Guidance flows for sustainable practices (step-by-step)
  • Compliance & subsidy tracking where applicable
Functions
  • Push advisory messages to grouped farmers (SMS/WhatsApp templates)
  • Schedule field demos and track attendance
  • Monitor adoption KPIs (regions, crops, adoption rate)
  • Generate simple policy briefs and printable leaflets
Interface Features
Advisory Builder

Template-based recommendations with localization for crop, language and practice.

Adoption Dashboard

Map + KPIs (adoption %, demo count) and simple export for reports.

Event Scheduler

Create demo events, capture attendance, feedback, and link back to farmer profiles.

Compliance Tracker

Track eligible farmers for subsidies and quick audit logs for inspections.

Developers & Data Scientists

Description: Teams maintaining apps, models and data pipelines focused on reliability and explainability.

Requirements
  • Access to labeled datasets and versioned model artifacts
  • Clear error logs and monitoring hooks (performance & data drift)
  • APIs and sample payloads for integration and testing
  • Non-technical dashboards for model performance to show stakeholders
Functions
  • Download labeled datasets and annotation ledger
  • Trigger model re-training and view job status
  • View logs, data drift alerts and sample misclassified cases
  • Provide lightweight SDKs or API endpoints for integration
Interface Features
Dataset Explorer

Filter by crop, label, reviewer, date. Preview images and metadata inline.

Model Playground

Upload sample input, view predictions, confidence scores and explanation highlights.

Monitoring

Simple alert center for model drift, latency spikes and error rates.

API Docs & SDKs

Copy-ready examples and sandbox keys (read-only for safety).

AgriTech Companies & Investors

Description: Product teams and investors focusing on funding, distribution, and scaling market reach.

Requirements
  • Market demand signals and farmer cohort analytics
  • Distribution performance and channel metrics
  • Return-on-investment estimates and pilot outcomes
  • Clear pricing templates and partnership models
Functions
  • View pilot dashboards and conversion funnels
  • Run simple revenue/ROI calculators for different roll-out plans
  • Download pitch-ready one-pagers and data snapshots
  • Connect with local partners and distribution agents
Interface Features
Pilot Performance

Conversion funnels, CAC, LTV estimates and regional heatmaps.

ROI Simulator

Interactive sliders for pricing, adoption rate and distribution cost to show break-even scenarios.

Partner Directory

Verified distributors, demo hosts and contact flows for onboarding.

Export Pack

Auto-generate presentation slides and brief data reports for investor meetings.

Environmental Organizations

Description: NGOs and advocacy groups aiming to reduce pesticide use and promote sustainable practices.

Requirements
  • Metrics for pesticide usage, biodiversity indicators, and soil health
  • Evidence-based alternatives and low-input practice guides
  • Tools to track the impact of interventions over time
  • Privacy-preserving farmer data sharing for research
Functions
  • Visualize pesticide application intensity over regions
  • Recommend eco-friendly alternatives and integrated pest management steps
  • Collect long-term impact data (soil tests, biodiversity surveys)
  • Facilitate anonymous reporting and consented data pools
Interface Features
Sustainability Map

Heatmap of pesticide intensity, conservation priority zones and intervention overlays.

Practice Library

Short how-to guides, video clips and farmer-friendly timing calendars to reduce chemical use.

Impact Tracker

Longitudinal charts for pesticide kg/ha, beneficial insect counts, and soil organic matter.

Data Consent Center

Tools to request, review and manage farmer data-sharing permissions and anonymization options.

Common UI Skeleton (for all stakeholders)

Design notes to ensure the product is friendly, accessible and practical for mixed audiences.

Principles
  • Plain language — avoid jargon; use short sentences and tooltips
  • Mobile-first — most users will access via basic smartphones
  • Low-bandwidth modes — compressed images, offline caches and SMS fallbacks
  • Accessibility — high contrast, keyboard support and readable fonts
